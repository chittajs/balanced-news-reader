# Balanced News Reader

A curated, non-personalized news reader. It shows the same event as covered across the
political spectrum — left, center, right, and state-affiliated — and across regions (US,
Europe, China, India, Australia, South America, Africa, Japan, Russia). The sources are
chosen by a person, not by an algorithm reacting to you.

**Open it:** https://chittajs.github.io/balanced-news-reader/

## Motivation

On the mainstream feeds — Google News, Yahoo, YouTube, MSN — the news is skewed toward
more of what an algorithm has decided you are. I'm not that. I read across opinions, hold
my own, and revise them. At scale the same personalization polarizes, and much of the
content filling those feeds is sloppy and generated in volume. This is the alternative I
wanted: pick the outlets, lay one story out across the spectrum, read the originals.

## How to use it

1. **Pick a topic.** Open the left rail (☰) and choose one — World & Politics, Economics,
   Trading & Markets, Science, Technology, Sports.
2. **Filter by region.** Each map pin is a region, sized by how many stories are in view
   there. Tap a pin to filter; tap again to clear.
3. **Scan the stories.** The list is ranked by how many independent outlets ran a story
   and how recent it is — nothing about you. A colored bar shows the spread of coverage
   across the spectrum.
4. **Expand a story.** Tap a card to see the outlets that covered it, grouped by lean:
   Left, Center, Right, and State-affiliated (kept separate, never blended). Each shows an
   ownership tag and a lock mark if paywalled.
5. **Read the original.** Tap ↗ to open the article on the publisher's own site.
6. **Hide paywalls (optional).** The toggle under the map removes subscription-only
   sources.
